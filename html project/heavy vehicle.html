Engines in buses and trucks are the powerhouse behind these heavy-duty vehicles, designed to move people and goods efficiently over long distances. These engines are usually internal combustion engines, predominantly diesel, due to their robust performance and fuel efficiency.



Types of Engine used in BUSES/TRUCKS


  • Diesel Engine

  • Gasoline Engine

  • Natural Gas Engine

  • Hybrid Engine

  • Electric Engine

  • Hydrogen Cell Engine

DIESEL ENGINE

The backbone of most buses and trucks, diesel engines are favored for their durability, fuel efficiency, and high torque output. They operate by compressing air to high temperatures, then injecting fuel to ignite and drive the engine. Examples include Cummins, Caterpillar, and Detroit Diesel engines.

GASOLINE

Less common in heavy-duty applications due to lower fuel efficiency and torque compared to diesel engines. Typically found in lighter trucks and older buses.

NATURAL GAS ENGINE

These engines use compressed natural gas (CNG) or liquefied natural gas (LNG) as fuel. They are cleaner-burning than diesel, reducing emissions and operating costs. Examples include the Cummins Westport and ISL G engines.

HYBRID ENGINE

Combine a traditional diesel or gasoline engine with an electric motor. These engines reduce fuel consumption and emissions, especially useful in urban settings with frequent stop-and-go traffic. The Allison H 40/50 EP is an example.

ELECTRIC ENGINE

Fully electric powertrains are becoming more viable with advancements in battery technology. These engines are quiet, efficient, and emit no tailpipe pollutants. Examples include those used in the BYD K9 electric bus and the Tesla Semi.
